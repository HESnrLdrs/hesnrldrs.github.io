How I Got Here

A simple email asking for career transition help brought me out of retirement post-COVID. That conversation reminded me why this work has mattered throughout my career - and led me to develop new tools for the challenges facing today's senior professionals.

It started in the early 1970s when, fresh out of Britannia Royal Naval College as a new lieutenant, I was appointed to HMS Excellent as an Instructor Lieutenant. What I didn't realise was that amongst my duties would be serving as Naval Resettlement Officer - not because I'd chosen the role, but because that's what Instructor Lieutenants did.

Suddenly I was helping officers and ratings work out civilian life after military service. I was learning as much as they were about career transitions, identity shifts, and what it means to translate professional skills into new contexts.

Fifty years later, after working with senior academics across 50+ UK universities through major institutional changes, I find myself having remarkably similar conversations. The contexts change, but the fundamental challenges of career transition remain remarkably consistent.

What I've Learnt

Career transitions aren't just about updating CVs or networking. They're about identity, strategic thinking, and understanding how your deep capabilities translate to new environments. Most career support either treats academics like corporate executives (missing the identity complexity) or offers generic advice (ignoring analytical strengths).

Senior professionals - whether leaving the military or considering life beyond academia - need frameworks that respect their intellectual rigour whilst addressing their particular challenges.

Coming 2026

Book Project: Memoir & The NAVIGATE Framework

I'm currently documenting the journey from that first reluctant appointment as Naval Resettlement Officer through five decades of supporting career transitions. The memoir weaves together personal experiences with the development of practical frameworks - culminating in the NAVIGATE tool that synthesises what I've learned about helping people through complex career decisions.

The book will include the complete NAVIGATE methodology, detailed case studies (appropriately anonymised), and the strategic frameworks that have proven most effective for senior professionals facing career transitions.

What NAVIGATE Includes

While I'm still finalising the framework, NAVIGATE addresses the systematic approach to career transition that respects both the analytical capabilities and emotional complexity of senior professional identity shifts. It's designed specifically for strategic thinkers who want rigorous frameworks rather than generic career advice.

Expected publication: 2025. Updates will be shared with those interested in following the project.

Working Together

I work selectively with a small number of senior academic and healthcare leaders who are navigating career transitions.

This isn't career coaching or life coaching in the traditional sense. It's experienced guidance from someone who understands both the strategic challenges of professional leadership and the personal complexity of identity shifts that accompany major career changes.

I typically work with 4-6 individuals at any time, allowing for the depth of conversation and strategic thinking that complex transitions require.

If you're a senior professional considering your options, I'd welcome a brief conversation about your situation.
