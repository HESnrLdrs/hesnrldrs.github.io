What Is the Free Toolkit?

The Free Career Transition Toolkit is a comprehensive collection of evidence-based assessment tools specifically designed for senior academics and leaders in higher education. These assessments provide immediate, actionable insights to support your career transition decisions and professional development.

Developed by Paul Thomas, a transformation specialist with an MA in Change, these tools combine proven psychological frameworks with practical guidance tailored to the unique challenges faced by senior academic professionals.

How It Helps You

Each assessment in the toolkit addresses a specific aspect of career transition and leadership development:

  • Clarity and Self-Awareness: Understand your natural preferences, strengths, and areas for development
  • Strategic Decision-Making: Gain frameworks for thinking through complex career choices
  • Transition Readiness: Assess your preparedness for significant career changes
  • Sustainable Balance: Evaluate and improve your work-life integration
  • Resilience Building: Develop strategies for thriving under academic pressures

Research Foundation

The toolkit assessments are grounded in established research from organisational psychology, career development theory, and change management literature:

Support Style Assessment: Based on leadership support research and communication preference studies, helping you understand how you naturally provide and receive support in professional relationships.

Career Transition Navigator: Incorporates Bridges' Transition Model and Schlossberg's Transition Theory, providing a research-backed framework for navigating career changes with confidence.

Life Balance Scorecard: Draws from work-life integration research and the concept of sustainable high performance, helping you create a balanced approach to demanding academic roles.

Academic Resilience Assessment: Based on psychological resilience research and stress management studies, specifically adapted for the unique pressures of academic leadership environments.
