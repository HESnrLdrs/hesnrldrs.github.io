What to Expect from Your Discovery Call

This is a professional consultation designed to understand your unique situation and explore how I can best support your career transformation goals.

Duration: 45 minutes
Format: Video call (Zoom) or telephone
Cost: Completely free
Commitment: None required

During our call, we'll explore:

  • Your current situation and what's driving your interest in change
  • Your goals, aspirations, and vision for your future
  • Any challenges or constraints you're currently facing
  • How my coaching approach could support your transformation
  • Whether there's a good fit for working together
  • Next steps and available options for your development

Complete confidentiality guaranteed. Everything we discuss remains strictly between us, and there's no pressure to commit to any programme.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens after I book a discovery call?

I'll contact you within 24 hours to arrange a convenient time for our call. You'll receive a calendar invitation with video call details and a brief preparation guide to help you get the most from our conversation.

Is there any cost or commitment?

The discovery call is completely free with no strings attached. It's an opportunity for both of us to explore whether there's a good fit for working together. There's no pressure to commit to any programme.

How confidential is our conversation?

Everything we discuss is strictly confidential. I understand the sensitive nature of career transitions in academia and maintain the highest standards of professional discretion.

What if I'm not sure about making changes yet?

That's perfectly normal and exactly why the discovery call exists. Many of my clients start by simply exploring their options. There's no expectation that you need to have made any decisions before we talk.

Do you work with academics outside the UK?

Yes, I work with senior academics globally. All coaching sessions can be conducted via video call, and the fundamental challenges facing academic leaders are remarkably similar across different higher education systems.

What's your background in supporting academics?

I've been supporting academic professionals since the early 1990s, with particular expertise in career transitions, leadership development, and work-life balance. You can learn more about my background on the About Paul page.
